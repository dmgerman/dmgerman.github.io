Camille's, the Best of 38 Coffee Places and Restaurants in Victoria in on Month

Once in a while I feel a bit tired of the places where I eat lunch, then I have the urge to try new ones (and the ones I haven’t visited for sometime).

I think we, as humans, tend to be build habits and become repetitive and boring over time. After few years back, when I decided to try every restaurant in Victoria (and got close to my goal), I have gotten used to the same restaurants.

So I decided for one month to try new ones. Keep in mind. These are places where I had lunch or coffee. There rankings do not reflect their dinner service. I believe we evaluate lunch time in a very different way that we evaluate dinner time. Lunch is more utilitarian, while dinner is more about the event.

The big winner: Camille’s

Most of the best restaurants do not open at lunch time (e.g. The Brasserie, Ulla, and many others). It was welcoming news to see Camille’s open for lunch. I have never eaten there. It was just the second day it was open. I really, really hope they stay open at lunch time. It is some of the most sophisticated lunch time food I had in a long time in Victoria.

The best new restaurant: Effes

Ok, not so new. It has been there for few months now. Simple kebabs, but delicious. It is a great place to stop for a snack any time of the day (although they run out of meats in the afternoon). It is located to the right of the Odeon cineplex on Yates.

The disappointments

  • I will never be back at the Bard and the Banker .

  • Ça Va was the biggest disappointment. In my opinion, it is not worth it at lunch time.

Other surprises:

  • Regardless of how bad the coffee is, the Oak Bay Maria Coffee Shop is a great destination. Sadly, location beats quality when it comes to a coffee.

  • My thai place was much better than I remembered it.

  • I really, really enjoy eating at Aura. The view is great.
